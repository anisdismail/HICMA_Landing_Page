We would like to thank our collaborators for providing permission to use the images in our dataset.

Chester Beatty Library

We would like to thank the Chester Beatty Library (Chester Beatty, Dublin. CC BY-NC 4.0) for providing us access to the Ibn al-Bawwab Qur'an and permission to process and publish the images as part of the HICMA dataset. See digitized object.

Free Islamic Calligraphy

We would like to thank FreeIslamicCalligraphy, a non-profit website owned by the Prince Ghazi Trust for Qur'anic Thought, for providing us access to their library of hand-written Arabic calligraphy paintings.

Dr. Vahid Behmardi

Lastly, we would like to thank Dr. Vahid Behmardi, Associate Professor of Arabic and Persian Literature Chairperson, Department of Communication, Arts & Languages at the Lebanese American University, for his generous offer of original Arabic manuscripts.
